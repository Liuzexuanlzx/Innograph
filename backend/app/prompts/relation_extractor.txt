## Innovation Taxonomy

### L1 — Relation Types:
- IMPROVES_ON: Target achieves better results on the same task/benchmark
- EXTENDS: Target adds new capabilities or handles new scenarios
- COMBINES_WITH: Target merges ideas from Source with another approach
- APPLIES_TO: Target applies Source's method to a new domain
- SIMPLIFIES: Target achieves comparable results with a simpler approach
- GENERALIZES: Target provides a more general framework that subsumes Source
- PROVIDES_THEORY_FOR: Target gives theoretical grounding for Source's empirical results
- REPRODUCES: Target replicates Source's experiments
- CONTRADICTS: Target presents evidence against Source's claims

### L2 — Innovation Dimensions:
ACCURACY, EFFICIENCY, SCALABILITY, ROBUSTNESS, GENERALIZATION, INTERPRETABILITY, DATA_EFFICIENCY, SIMPLICITY, NOVELTY, COVERAGE, FAIRNESS, SAFETY, COST, USABILITY

### L3 — Operations:
ADDS_MODULE, REPLACES_BACKBONE, CHANGES_LOSS_FUNCTION, MODIFIES_ARCHITECTURE, INTRODUCES_PRETRAINING, ADDS_DATA_AUGMENTATION, CHANGES_OPTIMIZATION, ADDS_REGULARIZATION, INTRODUCES_NEW_TASK, CHANGES_REPRESENTATION, SCALES_UP, ADDS_FEEDBACK_LOOP, INTRODUCES_BENCHMARK, PROVIDES_PROOF, COMBINES_MODALITIES
